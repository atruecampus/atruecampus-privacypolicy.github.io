1) Legal Basis & Scope

We process data in line with India’s Information Technology Act, 2000, the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 (SPDI Rules), and principles under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act). This Policy applies to Institutions (colleges/universities), their administrators, staff, students, parents/guardians (where applicable), and visitors.

2) Definitions

  • Personal Information (PI): Information that identifies an individual (alone or in combination), e.g., name, email, mobile.
  • Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI): As per SPDI Rules (passwords, financial, health, biometrics, etc.).
  • Institution: A recognized educational body using AtrueCampus.
  • Controller / Decision Maker: For most education records, the Institution decides the purposes/means; we act as service provider/processor, except where we determine purposes independently (security, compliance, fraud prevention).

3) What We Collect

  • Account & Identity: name, date of birth/age, gender (optional), email, mobile, role, Institution, course/batch.
  • Education & Campus Data: enrolment, timetable, attendance, assignments, assessments, fees/payments, library/inventory, certificates, approvals, and related workflows.
  • Communications: in-app messages, notices, announcements, feedback, support.
  • Device & Usage: IP, device identifiers, OS/browser/app version, language, crash logs, diagnostics, feature usage, referral data.
  • Files: photos, PDFs, signatures, IDs, letterheads, documents uploaded by you/Institution.
  • Payments (if enabled): transaction metadata (amount, status, time, masked instrument). We do not store full card/bank credentials.

4) How We Use Data

  • Provide and improve Platform features (attendance, timetables, assessments, certificates, notices, library, fees, reports).
  • Authenticate users, enforce role-based access, and prevent unauthorized/fraudulent use.
  • Enable communications (alerts, emails/SMS, push) for legitimate educational activities.
  • Personalize experiences and generate analytics/aggregated insights (non-identifying).
  • Support, debugging, and maintaining security/integrity of the Platform.
  • Comply with law, enforce terms, protect rights, safety, and property.

5) Lawful Grounds

Contract with you/Institution; consent; legitimate uses in an educational context; legal obligations; and vital/public interest (e.g., security incident response).

6) Sharing & Disclosures

  • Institution & Authorized Users: Within your Institution per role-based access and its directions.
  • Vendors/Processors: Hosting, analytics, crash reporting, messaging, payments, storage, identity, security (e.g., Google Play Services; Firebase Analytics/Crashlytics/Cloud Messaging/App Check; reCAPTCHA; payment gateways). Bound by contracts; process only on our instructions.
  • Legal/Compliance: Courts/regulators/law enforcement when required or to protect rights/safety.
  • Corporate Events: Merger/acquisition/asset transfer under this Policy’s protections.

7) International Transfers

Data may be processed on servers or by vendors outside your state/country. We take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards and transfer only where permitted.

8) Security

  • Reasonable technical/organizational measures: encryption in transit, access controls, least-privilege, audit logs, backups, monitoring.
  • No system is 100% secure. If a breach occurs, we’ll notify affected parties/authorities as legally required.

9) Anti-Misuse & Role Obligations

  • Admins must grant access strictly on need-to-know; revoke promptly when roles change.
  • Prohibited: downloading/scraping/exporting/sharing personal data outside legitimate academic/administrative purposes without legal basis and consent.
  • We may suspend/terminate accounts and notify Institutions/authorities for suspected abuse, impersonation, or unauthorized access.

10) Children, Students & Parents / Guardians

The Platform supports institutions of all levels including pre-primary, primary, secondary, higher-secondary, and higher-education institutions. Parents or guardians may be allowed to access their child’s records and updates on the Platform when such access is enabled or approved by the Institution. The Institution (school / college) is responsible for controlling account creation, assigning access roles, and obtaining & managing required parental/guardian consent for minors where applicable. Anyone submitting or managing a minor’s information confirms that they have lawful authority to do so.

11) Your Rights

Subject to law and institutional policies, you may request access, correction, update, deletion, or withdraw consent. Some requests must be routed via your Institution (controller of education records). We may retain certain data where required by law, for disputes, or to enforce agreements.

12) Data Retention

We retain data as needed for the purposes above or per Institution contracts/legal requirements. Archived records may be kept up to seven (7) years (or longer if mandated). Aggregated/de-identified data may be retained indefinitely.
